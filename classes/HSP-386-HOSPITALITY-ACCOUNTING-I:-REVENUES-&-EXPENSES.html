HSP 386 HOSPITALITY ACCOUNTING I: REVENUES & EXPENSES

The application of accounting principles as utilized in the hospitality industry is explored. The purpose of this class is to advance the hospitality student's knowledge of accounting concepts related to the income statement using the hospitality industry version of the Uniform System of Accounts. The class increases the student's understanding of the importance of revenue recognition, expense classification, and matching of revenue and expenses for operational analysis purposes. Students will study payroll accounting, withholding and tax reporting requirements and regulations specific to the hospitality industry along with labor variances and their relevance to understanding operations and performance versus budgets.
